## For Businesses

### Operating in France

| Factor | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| Infrastructure reliability | Declining; plan for disruption |
| Labour relations | Conflict-prone; strikes likely |
| Regulatory burden | EU plus French overlay |
| Market size | Contracting |

### Strategic Choices

- Maintain minimal footprint
- Invest in automation to reduce labour exposure
- Ensure supply chain redundancy
- Plan for extended disruption scenarios

## For Investors

### Avoid

- French public debt (fiscal trajectory negative)
- French retail banking
- French commercial real estate in vulnerable areas
- French-exposed business services

### Consider

- Short positions on French indices during unrest episodes
- Companies relocating from France
- Alternative European markets (Poland, Netherlands)

## Urgency Assessment

| Factor | UK | France |
|--------|----|----|
| Economic decline | Severe | Severe |
| Social unrest risk | High | High |
| State capacity | Weak | Moderate |
| Exit difficulty | Post-Brexit | EU freedom of movement |

France retains EU freedom of movement, making exit easier than UK. However, the window for orderly relocation narrows as crisis develops.
