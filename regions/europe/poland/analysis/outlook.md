# Poland Outlook: 2026-2036

> **Classification**: Winner (European context)
> **GDP Projection**: +3 to +4% annually
> **Confidence**: Moderate

## Core Assessment

Poland is the most surprising positive case in Europe. The combination of lower financial services exposure, growing manufacturing base, demographic stability, and ideological independence creates a genuinely different trajectory than Western European peers.

## Why Winner Status

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| Financial services exposure | Low—avoids first wave AI disruption |
| Manufacturing base | Growing—provides alternative employment |
| Demographics | Younger, more homogeneous |
| Ideological capture | Avoided |
| Energy approach | Pragmatic diversification |
| American security relationship | Strong |

## Limitations

Poland's positive trajectory is *relative* to Western Europe, not absolute:
- Still subject to EU regulatory framework
- Energy costs still higher than US
- Data centre capacity still modest
- Limited domestic AI companies
- Dependent on external investment

Poland does better because neighbours do worse, not because Poland becomes a technology leader.
