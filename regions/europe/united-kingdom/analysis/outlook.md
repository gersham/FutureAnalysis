> **Classification**: Fracturing
> **Growth Outlook**: Negative with instability risk

## Core Assessment

The United Kingdom represents the central case for Western fracture. Every vulnerability compounds. This is not decline; it is structural breakdown.

## Scenarios

### Base Case (-4% annually, fracturing)
- Financial services contract 30-40% by 2035
- Multiple episodes of serious civil unrest
- Infrastructure attacks occur
- Standard of living declines 20-30%
- Scotland holds independence referendum

### Optimistic Case (-2% annually, declining but stable)
- Managed decline like Japan
- Social cohesion proves more resilient than expected
- Political realignment produces competent governance
- Unrest contained at lower levels

### Pessimistic Case (-6% annually, severe fracturing)
- Financial collapse more rapid
- Civil unrest severe and sustained
- Scottish independence achieved
- Extended periods of emergency governance
- Significant emigration of skilled population
