### Tesla Optimus: Progress vs. Targets

**Capabilities demonstrated (October 2025)**:
- Optimus Gen 3 unveiled with Kung Fu sequences, cooking, household cleaning
- All learned autonomously through observation rather than explicit programming
- Qualitative leap from programmed movements to generalised learning

**Production reality check**:

| Metric | Target | Actual (Dec 2025) | Gap |
|--------|--------|-------------------|-----|
| 2025 production | 5,000-10,000 units | "Hundreds" (mid-2025 reports) | Significant shortfall |
| 2026 target | 50,000-100,000 units | — | At risk |
| Long-term target | 1 million/year | — | Aspirational |

**Challenges identified**:
- Supply chain issues and engineering challenges (internal sources, July 2025)
- Assembly at Gigafactory Texas still ramping
- November 2025: Announced plans for dedicated facility capable of 10M units/year by 2027 (highly ambitious)

**Assessment**: Demonstrations impressive; production significantly behind schedule. Musk's projection that humanoid robotics could account for 80% of Tesla's future value remains speculative. Target price of $20,000-$30,000 at mass production assumes manufacturing breakthroughs not yet achieved.

**Key uncertainty**: Chinese manufacturers (Unitree, BYD) may accelerate timeline through aggressive pricing, even as Western manufacturers struggle with production scale. The robotics wave may arrive faster in China than in US/Europe.

## Implications for Labor Markets

The robotics wave is **rear-weighted** toward late 2020s and 2030s:
- White-collar workers displaced by AI (2023-2030) cannot simply retrain for blue-collar work
- Blue-collar jobs face automation in the subsequent wave (2028-2035)
- Traditional "escape valve"—moving down the skill ladder—closes as physical automation scales
- Manual labor, service work, retail, hospitality face displacement in the second wave
